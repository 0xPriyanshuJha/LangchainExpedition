The Gettysburg Address, delivered by President Abraham Lincoln on November 19, 1863, is one of the most famous speeches in American history. In just 272 words, Lincoln honored the soldiers who fought at Gettysburg during the Civil War and redefined the purpose of the war. He emphasized the principles of equality, liberty, and democracy, stating that the nation was founded on the idea that all men are created equal. Lincoln's speech called for a new birth of freedom and reaffirmed the importance of a government of the people, by the people, for the people, which would endure despite the ongoing conflict.
